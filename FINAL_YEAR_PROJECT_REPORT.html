ABSTRACT


In the rapidly evolving landscape of the gig economy and small-medium enterprises (SMEs), efficient financial management is paramount. Traditional methods of invoicing—relying on paper trails, spreadsheets, or disjointed tools—often lead to errors, delayed payments, and poor record-keeping. Accountill is a modern, full-stack web application designed to solve these problems by providing a centralized platform for invoicing and client management.

Developed using the MERN Stack (MongoDB, Express.js, React, Node.js), Accountill offers a robust solution for freelancers and business owners. The application features a secure authentication system using JSON Web Tokens (JWT) and Google OAuth, ensuring user data privacy. Core functionalities include the creation, management, and tracking of professional invoices, receipts, estimates, and quotations. Users can customize documents, manage client databases, and visualize financial health through an interactive dashboard powered by Apex Charts.

Key innovations in this project include an automated PDF generation engine using html-pdf, a seamless email delivery system via Nodemailer for sending invoices directly to clients, and a real-time payment tracking system that updates invoice statuses automatically. This report documents the entire software development lifecycle of Accountill, from requirements elicitation and system design to implementation and testing, demonstrating a scalable architecture suitable for real-world deployment.

CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION

1.1 Introduction

The digital transformation of business processes has revolutionized how organizations operate, moving from manual, paper-based systems to sophisticated digital solutions. Among these processes, financial management—specifically invoicing and billing—remains a critical function for any specific business entity, regardless of its size. Accountill is a web-based application developed to streamline these operations. It leverages modern web technologies to provide a seamless, responsive, and secure platform for managing invoices, ensuring that businesses can operate efficiently in a digital-first world.

In the modern gig economy, the ability to manage finances effectively can determine the success or failure of a venture. Accountill aims to bridge the gap between complex enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems and simple manual tools, offering a balanced solution that is both powerful and user-friendly. By utilizing the MERN stack, the application benefits from a unified JavaScript development environment, ensuring high performance and scalability.

1.2 Background of the Study

Freelancers and Small-to-Medium Enterprises (SMEs) often struggle with administrative overheads. According to recent surveys, freelancers spend a significant portion of their non-billable time on administrative tasks, including creating invoices, following up on payments, and organizing client details. While large enterprises have access to expensive ERP solutions like SAP or Oracle, these are often cost-prohibitive and overly complex for smaller entities. This disparity creates a market need for accessible tools that provide professional invoicing capabilities without the steep learning curve or high costs associated with enterprise software.

Historically, small businesses relied on physical ledgers and triplicate invoice books. As technology advanced, this shifted to spreadsheets (e.g., Microsoft Excel) and word processors. While these digital tools offered improvements, they lacked automation, centralization, and specialized features such as payment tracking or automatic tax calculations. The evolution of web technologies now allows for cloud-based solutions like Accountill, which offer accessibility from any device with an internet connection, transforming how small businesses handle their day-to-day financial transactions.

1.3 Problem Statement

The current landscape of invoicing for small businesses faces several challenges that hinder growth and efficiency:

  1. Manual Errors and Inaccuracy: Using generic tools like Microsoft Word or Excel often requires manual entry of formulas. This leads to frequent calculation errors in taxes, discounts, and grand totals, which can result in financial losses or disputes with clients.
  2. Lack of Centralization and Organization: Client details, past invoices, and payment records are often scattered across different files, folders, and devices. This fragmentation makes it difficult to retrieve historical data or maintain a coherent audit trail.
  3. Inefficiency and Time Consumption: Manually converting documents to PDF, drafting emails, and attaching files for every single invoice is repetitive and time-consuming. This "busy work" takes valuable time away from core business activities.
  4. Poor Analytics and Financial Visibility: Without a dedicated system, tracking outstanding payments, analyzing monthly revenue trends, and identifying top clients becomes difficult. Business owners essentially operate "blind," lacking the data needed to make informed decisions.
  5. Data Security and Integrity: Local files stored on personal computers are susceptible to loss via hardware failure, corruption, or unauthorized access. They often lack proper backup mechanisms and encryption, posing a significant risk to confidential financial data.
  6. Professionalism and Branding: Inconsistent formatting, typos, and manual errors can damage the professional reputation of a business. A standardized, branded invoice builds trust and credibility with clients.

1.4 Significance of the Study

This project is significant as it addresses the specific needs of the underserved market of freelancers and SMEs. By providing a free, open-source alternative to paid subscriptions, it democratizes access to professional financial tools. It empowers small business owners to take control of their finances without incurring high costs.

From a technical perspective, the project serves as a comprehensive case study in full-stack web development. It demonstrates the practical application of the MERN stack in building complex, real-world applications. The modular architecture and use of modern libraries (Redux, Material-UI, JWT) serve as a valuable learning resource for developers and students interested in modern software engineering practices.

1.5 Objectives of the Project

The primary goal of Accountill is to design and develop a robust invoicing platform. The specific objectives are:

  • To Develop: A comprehensive full-stack web application capable of seamless CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete) operations for invoices, clients, and user profiles.
  • To Automate: The extensive calculation of sub-totals, generic tax rates (VAT), discounts, and grand totals to eliminate human error and ensure financial accuracy.
  • To Secure: Implement robust authentication and authorization mechanisms using industry standards like JWT and OAuth to ensure that a user's financial data is private, encrypted, and inaccessible to unauthorized parties.
  • To Integrate: Advanced features such as server-side PDF generation and automated email delivery services to streamline the workflow of sending invoices to clients.
  • To Visualize: Provide an interactive, graphical dashboard that summarizes financial data into actionable insights (e.g., Total Paid vs. Unpaid, Payment History) using data visualization libraries.

In-Scope:

  • User Management: Registration, Login, Google OAuth integration, and Profile management (Logo, Address, Contact Info updates).
  • Client Module: Adding new clients, editing client details, maintaining a client directory, and deleting obsolete records.

CHAPTER 2: LITERATURE REVIEW

2.1 Overview of the MERN Stack

The MERN stack is a state-of-the-art, JavaScript-based software stack for building dynamic web sites and web applications. It is an acronym for MongoDB, Express.js, React, and Node.js. The primary advantage of this stack is the unification of the language: JavaScript is used from the client-side to the server-side, and even in the database query language.

  • MongoDB: A document-oriented NoSQL database used to store application data. Unlike traditional SQL databases that use tables and rows, MongoDB uses flexible, JSON-like documents with dynamic schemas. This allows for faster iteration and easier handling of unstructured data, which is ideal for the variable structures often found in invoice line items.
  • Express.js: A minimal and flexible web application framework for Node.js. It simplifies the process of building robust APIs and handling HTTP requests. In Accountill, Express.js manages the routing, middleware (such as authentication checks), and server-side logic, acting as the bridge between the frontend and the database.
  • React: A declarative, efficient, and flexible frontend JavaScript library for building user interfaces. Maintained by Meta (Facebook), it allows for the creation of reusable UI components. Its "Virtual DOM" feature ensures that updates to the UI are performed efficiently, providing a smooth, app-like experience for the user without constant page reloads.
  • Node.js: A JavaScript runtime built on Chrome's V8 JavaScript engine. It allows developers to run JavaScript on the server side. Node.js is non-blocking and event-driven, making it highly scalable and suitable for data-intensive real-time applications like Accountill.

2.2 Comparison with Other Stacks

MERN vs. LAMP (Linux, Apache, MySQL, PHP): The LAMP stack has been the backbone of the web for decades. However, it relies on multiple languages (PHP for backend, SQL for database, JavaScript for frontend), which increases context switching for developers. MERN offers a unified language (JavaScript) for the entire stack, leading to better developer productivity, code reuse, and a more streamlined development process. Additionally, MongoDB's JSON-like structure maps directly to frontend objects, eliminating the "impedance mismatch" and the need for complex ORMs required in SQL-based LAMP stacks.

MERN vs. MEAN (Angular instead of React): Both stacks are very similar, differing primarily in the frontend framework. Angular is a full-fledged MVC framework that is highly opinionated, providing a specific way to structure applications. React, conversely, is a library focused only on the view layer. This offers greater flexibility and allows developers to choose their own libraries for routing and state management. React's Virtual DOM also generally provides superior performance for dynamic, high-interaction applications compared to Angular's real DOM manipulation in earlier versions.

MERN vs. Python/Django: Python frameworks like Django are known for their "batteries-included" approach. While excellent for rapid MVP development, they traditionally use synchronous processing. Node.js's asynchronous, event-driven architecture handles high concurrency (many users generating invoices simultaneously) more efficiently with fewer resources.

2.3 Related Technologies

In addition to the core stack, several other modern technologies were integrated to enhance functionality:

  • Redux: A predictable state container for JavaScript apps. It helps write applications that behave consistently, run in different environments (client, server, and native), and are easy to test. In Accountill, Redux is crucial for managing the complex global state, such as the current user's authentication status, the list of invoices loaded, and the transient state of an invoice being edited.
  • Material-UI (MUI): A popular React UI framework. It provides a robust set of ready-made components that follow Google's Material Design guidelines. This ensures a modern, clean, and accessible user interface out-of-the-box, significantly speeding up the UI development process and ensuring consistency across the application.
  • JWT (JSON Web Tokens): An open standard (RFC 7519) that defines a compact and self-contained way for securely transmitting information between parties as a JSON object. This information can be verified and trusted because it is digitally signed. Accountill uses JWT for stateless authentication, allowing the server to verify user identity without storing session data in a database.
  • Axios: A promise-based HTTP client for the browser and Node.js. It is used to make asynchronous HTTP requests to REST endpoints and perform CRUD operations. It offers features like interceptors, which are used in Accountill to automatically attach the authentication token to every outgoing request.

2.4 Review of Existing Systems

To understand the competitive landscape, several existing invoicing solutions were analyzed:

  • Zoho Invoice: A powerful commercial tool part of the Zoho suite. Strength: Deep customization and integration with other Zoho apps. Weakness: The free tier is very restrictive on the number of customers, and the interface can be overwhelming for simple needs.
  • Wave Apps: A popular free accounting tool. Strength: comprehensive double-entry accounting. Weakness: It has started restricting features in certain regions and often heavily markets paid payroll services to free users. Accountill offers a clutter-free experience focused solely on invoicing.
  • FreshBooks: A premium, paid service known for its user-friendly interface. Strength: Excellent time-tracking and expense management. Weakness: The monthly subscription cost is a barrier for many freelancers just starting out. Accountill aims to replicate the ease of use of FreshBooks but as a free, self-hosted option.

CHAPTER 4: SYSTEM DESIGN

4.1 System Architecture

The application uses a RESTful Client-Server Architecture coupled with a Single Page Application (SPA) frontend. This separation of concerns allows for independent scalability and easier maintenance.

  • Client Side (Frontend): Built with React.js, the client runs in the user's browser. It handles presentation, user interaction, and state management. It communicates with the server via HTTP/HTTPS requests (GET, POST, PUT, DELETE). Routing is handled client-side by `react-router-dom`, providing a seamless "no-refresh" navigation experience.
  • Server Side (Backend): Built with Node.js and Express.js, the server acts as the central controller. It exposes a set of API endpoints consumed by the client. It handles business logic, authentication (JWT verification), input validation, and communication with the database. It is also responsible for resource-intensive tasks like PDF generation and email dispatching.
  • Database Layer: MongoDB Cluster (Atlas) serves as the persistence layer. It stores all user, client, and invoice data. The connection is managed via Mongoose, an Object Data Modeling (ODM) library that provides schema validation and query building capabilities.

4.2 Database Design

The database consists of three primary collections. The schema design focuses on embedding related data where appropriate for read performance, while referencing data where normalization is better for updates.

4.2.4 Entity Relationship Description

The relationship between these entities is defined as follows:

  • User to Clients (1:N): One user can have multiple clients. Each client record belongs to exactly one user (enforced via `userId`).
  • User to Invoices (1:N): One user can create multiple invoices.
  • Client to Invoices (1:N): One client can be associated with multiple invoices over time. Note that in the Invoice schema, client data is often embedded rather than just referenced. This is a deliberate design choice in NoSQL to ensure that if a client's address changes in the future, old invoices retain the address that was valid at the time of invoicing, preserving historical accuracy.

5.2 Backend Implementation Deep Dive

The backend is structured using the Controller-Service-Repository pattern (simplified to Routes-Controllers-Models for this scope). This ensures a clean separation of concerns.

5.2.1 Authentication Controller Logic

The authentication system distinguishes between standard email/password login and Google OAuth. The `signin` controller handles the logic:

    
// controllers/user.js
export const signin = async (req, res) => {
    const { email, password } = req.body; // Extract credentials

    try {
        // 1. Check if user exists
        const existingUser = await User.findOne({ email });
        if(!existingUser) return res.status(404).json({ message: "User doesn't exist." });

        // 2. Validate Password
        const isPasswordCorrect = await bcrypt.compare(password, existingUser.password);
        if(!isPasswordCorrect) return res.status(400).json({ message: "Invalid credentials." });

        // 3. Generate JWT Token
        // The token contains the email and id, and expires in 1 hour
        const token = jwt.sign(
            { email: existingUser.email, id: existingUser._id }, 
            'test', // Secret key (should be in .env)
            { expiresIn: "1h" }
        );

        // 4. Return user data and token
        res.status(200).json({ result: existingUser, token });
    } catch (error) {
        res.status(500).json({ message: "Something went wrong." });
    }
}

5.4 PDF Generation & Email Logic

The PDF feature is a standout technical implementation. Instead of generating the PDF on the client-side (which can differ between browsers and lacks consistency), the server handles it to ensure a standardized output.

  1. Data Transmission: Frontend sends the full invoice object to the `POST /invoices` endpoint.
  2. Template Injection: The backend passes this data into a dedicated `documents/index.js` file. This file contains an HTML template literal function that dynamically injects values (e.g., `${invoice.client.name}`, `${invoice.total}`) into an HTML structure designed for A4 paper.
  3. Conversion: The `html-pdf` library takes this populated HTML string and renders it into a binary PDF buffer effectively using a headless browser engine.
  4. Handling: This buffer is then either written to a temporary file for attachment or piped directly into the HTTP response so the user can download it immediately.

CHAPTER 7: CONCLUSION AND FUTURE SCOPE

7.1 Conclusion

The Accountill project successfully demonstrates the power, flexibility, and efficiency of the MERN stack for building modern web applications. We have successfully designed, developed, and deployed a fully functional invoicing application that directly addresses real-world business problems faced by the gig economy. The system allows users to securely manage their clients, generate critical professional financial documents, and track their earnings with ease.

Through this project, the practical application of concepts such as REST API design, JWT authentication, State Management with Redux, and NoSQL database modeling has been validated. The final product achieves all primary objectives: it is secure, user-friendly, and technically robust.

7.2 Challenges Faced

  • Redux State Management: Managing the complex state of an invoice—which includes nested arrays of items, client objects, and metadata—was initially difficult. Syncing this state between the "Add Invoice" and "Edit Invoice" modes required careful planning of the Redux reducers.
  • PDF Styling: HTML-to-PDF conversion engines often render CSS differently than standard browsers. Achieving a pixel-perfect A4 layout required significant trial and error with specific print-media CSS queries.
  • Asynchronous Logic: Handling the timing between saving an invoice to the database and immediately generating a PDF for it (before the data might fully propagate in some eventual consistency models) required robust promise chaining.

7.3 Future Enhancements

While the current system is functional, there is significant room for growth. Future iterations plan to include:

  1. Payment Gateway Integration: Integrating Stripe or PayPal APIs to allow clients to "Pay Now" directly from the invoice PDF, automatically marking the invoice as "Paid" in the system.
  2. Multi-User Teams: Allowing a single company account to have multiple users (e.g., Administrator vs. Accountant) with different permission levels.
  3. Recurring Invoices: Implementing a background Cron job (using libraries like `node-cron`) to automatically generate and email invoices for subscription-based services on a monthly basis.
  4. Mobile Application: Developing a React Native mobile app that shares the same backend, allowing users to create invoices on-the-go from their smartphones.
